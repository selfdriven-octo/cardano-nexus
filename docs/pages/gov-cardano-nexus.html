// Why governance needs context

On-chain power, off-chain confusion

Conway-era governance gave ADA holders real on-chain power. But the context needed to use it well is scattered across forums, portals, threads and PDFs. Informed participation requires time, context and reflection — and a frantic ecosystem cannot govern wisely.

Governance actions are hard to understand without deep technical context
DRep rationales are scattered and rarely preserved
Voters react to timelines instead of reasoning from evidence
Constitutional context is hard to connect to live actions
Proposal dependencies and history are easily lost
Treasury outcomes are rarely linked back to delivery

// The lifecycle

From idea to enactment

Every action follows the same on-chain path. gov.cardano.nexus tracks each step and keeps the context attached to it.

01

Propose

Submitted on-chain with a deposit and CIP-100/108 metadata.

02

Discuss

Community review; rationales and risk analysis gather.

03

Vote

DReps, SPOs and the CC cast votes across the epoch window.

04

Ratify

If the required thresholds are met, the action is ratified.

05

Enact

Applied in the next epoch; the deposit is returned.

// Roadmap

Establishing a shared governance roadmap

Beyond tracking individual actions, gov.cardano.nexus helps the community propose, prioritise, track and remember governance priorities — a living roadmap the ecosystem can follow together. The platform itself rolls out in five phases, mapped to the 4Cs.

PHASE 1

Connect

curious

Index DReps, SPOs and the Constitutional Committee. Mirror live governance actions with neutral, plain-language explainers.

PHASE 2

Understand

curious

Attach constitutional context, risk analysis, dependency maps and clear voting timelines to every action.

PHASE 3

Participate

constructive

Calm digests and "what changed?" views, discussion spaces, and local governance groups — informed participation without the noise.

PHASE 4

Remember

caring

A durable rationale archive, voting history, and proposal-to-delivery evidence — so the ecosystem keeps its governance memory.

PHASE 5

Roadmap

chill

A community-curated, living governance roadmap: propose priorities, track progress across epochs, and open the schemas so anyone can build on the governance context graph.

// the chill mode
